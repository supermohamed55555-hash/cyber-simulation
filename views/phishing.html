What Happened?

You entered your credentials into a fake login form that was designed to look identical to a legitimate service. Instead of logging you in, the form sent your information to an attacker-controlled server.

Why it Worked?

  • Visual Mimicry: The form used the same fonts, colors, and logos as the real site.
  • Sense of Urgency: The prompt used "urgent" language to bypass your critical thinking.
  • Subtle URL Change: The domain social-connet.xyz looks very similar to socialconnect.com.

How to Prevent it?

  • Check the URL: Always verify the domain name character by character.
  • Look for SSL: Ensure the site has a valid HTTPS certificate (though even fake sites use HTTPS today).
  • Use MFA: Multi-Factor Authentication makes stolen passwords useless on their own.
  • Avoid Email Links: Navigate to sites directly by typing the URL and don't click on links in unexpected emails.
