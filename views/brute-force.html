Educational Breakdown

What Happened?

An automated script systematically tried every password from a "dictionary file" (a list of common or leaked passwords) against the login form. Since there were no protections, it eventually hit the correct password.

Why it Worked?

  • Weak Password Policy: The account used a simple, common password that was in the attacker's list.
  • No Rate Limiting: The server allowed thousands of requests per minute without blocking the attacker.
  • Lack of Account Lockout: The account remained active even after dozens of failed login attempts.

How to Prevent it?

  • Complex Passwords: Require long, unique passwords that aren't in common dictionary lists.
  • Account Lockout: Automatically disable accounts for a period (e.g., 30 mins) after 5 failed attempts.
  • Rate Limiting: Limit how many login attempts can be made from a single IP address per minute.
  • CAPTCHA: Require human verification after a few failed attempts.
